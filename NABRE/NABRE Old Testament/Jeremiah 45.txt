The word that Jeremiah the prophet spoke to Baruch, son of Neriah, when he wrote on a scroll words from Jeremiahs own mouth in the fourth year of Jehoiakim, son of Josiah, king of Judah:       Thus says the  Lord , God of Israel, to you, Baruch.     You said, Woe is me! the  Lord  has added grief to my pain.   I have worn myself out with groaning; rest eludes me.     You must say this to him. Thus says the  Lord : What I have built, I am tearing down; what I have planted, I am uprooting: all this land.       And you, do you seek great things for yourself? Do not seek them!
