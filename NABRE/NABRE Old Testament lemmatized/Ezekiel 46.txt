everyone shall leave by the opposite gate .      when they come in , the prince shall be with they ; he shall also leave with they .      on feast and festival , the grain offering shall be an ephah for a bull , an ephah for a ram , but for the lamb whatever they please , and a hin of oil with each ephah .      when the prince make a freewill offering to the   Lord , whether a burn offering or communion offering , the gate face east shall be open for he , and he shall bring his burn offering or peace offering as he do on the sabbath . then he shall leave , and the gate shall be close after his departure .       every day you shall bring as a burn offering to the   Lord   an unblemished year - old lamb ; you shall offer it every morning ,      and with it every morning a grain offering of one sixth of an ephah , with a third of a hin of oil to moisten the fine flour . this grain offering for the   Lord   be a perpetual statute .       the lamb , the grain offering , and the oil you must bring every morning as a perpetual burn offering .    the Prince and the Land .     thus say the Lord   God : if the prince make a gift of part of his heritage to any of his son , it belong to his son ; that property be their heritage .      but if he make a gift of part of his heritage to one of his servant , it belong to he until the year of release ;    then it revert to the prince . only the heritage give to his son belong to he .        the prince shall not seize any part of the heritage of the people by force they off their property . from his own property he shall provide heritage for his son , so that none of my people will be drive off their property .    the Temple Kitchens .     then he bring I through the entrance at the side of the gateway to the chamber reserve for the priest , which face north . there I see a place at the far west end ,      about which he say to I , this be the place where the priest cook the reparation offering and the purification offering and bake the grain offering , so they do not have to bring they into the outer court and so transmit holiness to the people .         then he lead I into the outer court and have I cross to the four corner of the court , and there , in each corner , be another court !      in all four corner of the courtyard there be court set off , each forty cubit long by thirty cubit wide , all four of they the same size .      a stone wall surround they on four side , and oven be build along the bottom of the wall all the way around .      he say to I , these be the kitchen where the temple minister cook the sacrifice of the people .
